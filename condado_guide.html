🍺 Drinking on the streets
🚫
No glass bottles in public. On the beach or streets, keep it in a can or plastic cup — glass is prohibited and police can fine you on the spot.
🏖️
Beach drinking is generally tolerated in cans or plastic. Condado, Ocean Park, and Isla Verde are relaxed about it — just no glass and clean up after yourself.
🚶
Street drinking depends on the municipality. Each town sets its own rules. Some areas like La Placita (Santurce) are zoned for it; most sidewalks technically aren't. Police discretion varies.
🔞
Drinking age is 18 — not 21 like the mainland. ID is often checked at bars and restaurants.
🚗
Zero tolerance in cars. Open containers in the passenger area are illegal — $100+ fine. Keep bottles in the trunk.
🚗 Driving — what's different from the US
🚦
Right turn on red is allowed unless a sign says otherwise.
🚔
Police lights on = not always pulling you over. PR police drive with lights on all the time. Only pull over if there's also a siren.
🌙
After midnight, you can roll through red lights with caution if the intersection is clear — locals do it to avoid sitting alone at dark intersections.
🛣️
Speed limits in MPH, distances in kilometers. Urban: 25 mph · Rural: 45 mph · Highway: 65 mph. Gas prices are per liter.
🔄
Left lane = fast lane on highways. Slower traffic stays right.
💳
Highways have tolls. Get an AutoExpreso pass from your rental company or pay cash at select lanes.
🍺
DUI limit is 0.08% BAC — same as the US mainland. Checkpoints common on weekends. First offense: $500 fine + 6-month license suspension.
📵
No phone while driving unless hands-free. Speed cameras active island-wide.
